Pizza

Pizza (Italian: ['pittisa'], Neapolitan: ['pittisa'] is a savory dish of Italian origin, consisting of a round, flattened base of leavened wheat-based dough topped with tomatoes, cheese, and often various other ingredients (such as anchovies, mushrooms, peppers, onions, olives, meat, etc.), which is then baked at high temperature, traditionally in a wood-fired oven.[1] A small pizza is sometimes called a "pizzeta", after the Italian queen Margherita of Savoy. A person who makes pizza is known as a "pizza maker" or "pizzaiolo".

Pizza is a beloved, versatile Italian dish featuring a baked dough base topped with savory tomato sauce, mozzarella cheese, and various toppings. Originating in Naples, it has become a global favorite due to its customizable nature, ranging from thin-crust Margherita to thick-crust pepperoni. Its comforting aroma and taste make it ideal for any meal.
